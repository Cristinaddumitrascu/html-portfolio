🕹 Real use cases

Work that actually happened

A look at specific challenges I've tackled, decisions I've made, and what changed as a result.

The problem
  • Retention was low from day one — the game had been live for 3 years but the core loop wasn't pulling players back.
  • A 3-stage onboarding forced players to return after several hours for a third session that felt identical to the first two — repetitive and forgettable.
  • Despite being a collaborative game, new players felt completely alone. No visible community, no sense of belonging.
  • Core mechanics were too passive — a single button did most of the work, removing any sense of skill or agency.
What I did
  • Collapsed the 3-stage onboarding into a single, focused session — removing repetition and getting players to the fun faster.
  • Redesigned core mechanics to introduce skill-based elements, giving players real agency over outcomes.
  • Built a team matchmaking system that placed new players into active teams on day one — teams were filtered by daily match participation and chat activity, so new players immediately felt part of something.
  • Designed and launched competitive systems that created ongoing engagement drivers beyond the core loop.
My role

Wearing three hats simultaneously: Producer coordinating a team of 15+ across dev, art, QA, marketing and support; Game Designer owning the onboarding and core loop redesign; and Data Analyst using player behaviour data to identify where and why players were dropping off. I owned the problem end-to-end, from diagnosis to shipped solution.

What changed
  • Players no longer experienced onboarding as a waiting game — the single-stage flow removed friction and reduced early drop-off.
  • The social layer transformed the first-session experience from solitary to communal, directly addressing the "I'm playing alone" perception.
  • Skill-based mechanics gave experienced players a reason to improve, not just repeat.
  • New competitive systems created regular engagement spikes and reasons to return beyond the core loop.
The problem
  • Retention was underperforming at D7 through D30, with early signals pointing to engagement drop-off well before players reached meaningful content.
  • Monetisation was not converting — the game lacked segmented IAP systems tailored to different player types and spending behaviours.
  • Players were reaching the end of available levels and leaving, with no systems to keep them engaged beyond the content wall.
  • Economy inflation was eroding the value of in-game rewards, reducing the incentive to progress.
  • Onboarding contained unnecessary friction steps that slowed down the path to the core loop.
  • Despite strong install volumes, retention metrics showed high volatility with no consistent growth direction — signalling systemic product issues rather than UA problems.
What I did
  • Designed and implemented a push notification system structured around player behaviour and lifecycle, not volume. Non-intrusive, high-relevance.
  • Built a collectibles system to combat economy inflation, creating new value layers without disrupting existing reward structures.
  • Stripped onboarding of all friction points, creating a faster, more logical flow that gets players to the fun sooner.
  • Designed match-3 specific LiveOps events calibrated to the genre's engagement rhythms and player psychology.
  • Rebuilt the global difficulty curve to reduce unintended churn spikes and improve long-term progression feel.
  • Created segmented IAP systems based on player type and spending behaviour, designed to move players across customer classes and improve conversion rate.
  • Defined and drove the product vision at exec level, translating data-backed insights into a multi-quarter roadmap presented to CEO and leadership.
My role

Head of Product leading a cross-functional team of artists, game designers, level designers, developers and data analysts. I set the product vision, own the roadmap, coordinate execution across disciplines, and translate business goals into product decisions. I operate at both the strategic and hands-on level, from exec presentations to sprint-level prioritisation. The focus throughout was on building systems that compound — improvements designed to move all retention metrics in the same direction, simultaneously.

What changed
  • D1 retention grew by +143% over the engagement period, from 15.35% to 37.36%.
  • D7 retention reached its all-time high of 12.42%, nearly double the starting point of 6.43%.
  • D30 retention improved from 2.8% to 4.2% with no reversals — consistent upward movement every month in Q4 2025.
  • All three retention metrics grew simultaneously for the first time in the product's tracked history.
  • Post-holiday churn was eliminated — 50% more players retained in January 2026 compared to January 2025, the hardest month in the calendar for mobile games.
  • Yearly D1 average lifted by 37% year-over-year, from 23.9% in 2024 to 32.7% in 2025.
  • D1 retention sustained above 35% for 4 consecutive months — a performance level never previously reached on this title.
